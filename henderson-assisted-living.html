Independent Living vs. Assisted Living in Henderson

Many Henderson families ask: "Should Mom move to independent or assisted living?" The answer depends on your parent's health, mobility, and social needs. Here's the key difference:

Independent Living (IL) Assisted Living (AL)
ADLs Supported None; resident is fully independent 2+ daily living tasks (bathing, dressing, meds, toileting)
Staffing Administrative; rarely medical Licensed staff, RN on duty during business hours (often 24/7)
Typical Cost (Henderson) $2,200–$3,000/mo $3,400–$4,500/mo
Housing Style Apartment or cottage; no medical equipment Private room/suite with call button, grab bars, wider doorways
Who It's For Active seniors who want community + independence Seniors with early dementia, mobility loss, medication management needs
Aging in Place? Limited; may require transition to AL later Yes; can typically stay through mid-stage dementia

Pro tip: Many Henderson communities offer both IL and AL under one roof (sometimes called "continuum of care"). This lets your parent start in IL and transition to AL as needs change — without uprooting. Ask about this during your tour.
