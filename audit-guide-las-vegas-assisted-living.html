The 10-Minute 'Smell Test' & Ratio Audit

How to vet any Las Vegas assisted living facility on your own, without relying on the sales brochure.

Touring a senior living community in Henderson or Las Vegas can be overwhelming. The lobby looks like a luxury hotel, the cookies are fresh, and the sales director is incredibly kind. But a lobby doesn't provide care at 3:00 AM.

In 2026, as staffing shortages hit the Clark County area, families are reporting a "Quality Trap"—facilities with high star ratings that actually have dangerous resident-to-staff ratios. Here is how to audit the truth in 10 minutes.

1. The 'Smell Test' (Beyond the Lobby)

Don't stop at the model room. Ask to see a room in the back of the building. Does it smell like heavy industrial floral cleaner? That is often used to mask underlying issues with hygiene or plumbing. A truly clean facility should smell... like nothing.

2. Use the "Ratio Audit"

The Pro Audit Question: Don't ask "What is your ratio?" Ask: "How many caregivers are assigned to this wing right now, and what is the current census (occupancy) of this wing?"

If they have 2 caregivers for 24 residents, that is a 1:12 ratio. In 2026, for a high-needs assisted living wing, anything over 1:8 is a red flag for response times.

3. The Call Light Test

As you walk the halls, listen. Do you hear call lights chiming for more than 5 minutes? Watch the staff: do they look frantic and overwhelmed, or are they moving at a calm, controlled pace? Calm staff usually means the facility is correctly tiered and staffed for the care levels they've accepted.

Summary

A beautiful building is nice, but care quality is defined by the people. Always perform your own ratio audit and trust your senses over the marketing materials. If a facility has "bait and switch" ratios (high on day 1, low on day 30), it's time to look elsewhere.
