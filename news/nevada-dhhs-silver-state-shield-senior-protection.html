Nevada DHHS Launches 'Silver State Shield' Senior Protection Program

2026-03-26 · local

Nevada's Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) has officially launched 'Silver State Shield,' a comprehensive program designed to protect Nevada's aging population from healthcare-related fraud and identity theft in assisted living environments.

Combating Healthcare Fraud

The program provides dedicated investigators who work directly with facility administrators to audit billing practices and ensure that state-funded care reaches the residents who need it most. It also includes education for family members on how to spot the signs of financial exploitation.

Expanded Care Access

Alongside the security measures, DHHS has announced a 15% increase in subsidies for low-income seniors seeking placement in high-rated assisted living facilities. This move is aimed at reducing the waitlist for specialized memory care and geriatric services across the state.
