Assisted Living Las Vegas 2026

Cost overview, best areas for senior living, and how Las Vegas compares to Henderson and Reno.

2026 Las Vegas Assisted Living Cost Overview

Assisted living Las Vegas and the broader metro typically run slightly above the Nevada state average because of demand and resort-style options. As of early 2026, families can expect average monthly assisted living costs in the Las Vegas area (including the Strip corridor and nearby suburbs) in the $3,800–$4,200 range, with memory care often $1,000–$1,500 more. For a lower-cost option in the same region, Henderson assisted living often comes in around $3,650/month and is only a short drive away.

Best Areas for Senior Living in Las Vegas

If you're searching for senior living Las Vegas or the best neighborhoods for assisted living, consider these 2026 standouts:

  • Summerlin: Master-planned community with strong medical and senior services; many facilities offer both independent and assisted tiers.
  • Southwest Las Vegas (Spring Valley, etc.): Good mix of mid-range and upscale options with easy access to hospitals.
  • Henderson (adjacent): Often better value than central Las Vegas; see our Henderson guide for costs and neighborhoods like Green Valley Ranch.

How Las Vegas Compares to Henderson and Reno

Southern Nevada offers two main hubs: Las Vegas proper and Henderson. Las Vegas assisted living tends to have more variety in terms of size and style (large campuses, high-rises), while Henderson is known for slightly lower average costs and strong senior-focused neighborhoods. Northern Nevada is different altogether: Reno and Sparks offer cooler weather and a focus on respiratory and specialty care—see our Vegas vs. Reno comparison for the full picture.
